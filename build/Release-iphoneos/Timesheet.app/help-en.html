Employer WorkWeek

An employer’s workweek is a fixed and regularly recurring period of 168 hours - seven consecutive 24-hour periods. It need not coincide with the calendar week, but may begin on any day and at any hour of the day. It is the employer’s responsibility to set the workweek.

An employee’s schedule may not coincide with an employer’s workweek. For example, an employee may work Monday through Friday but an employer’s workweek actually begins on a Sunday.

Gross Pay

Total regular wages due to an employee including overtime pay, commissions, and non-discretionary bonuses before any deductions are made. Deductions can include federal and state taxes and wage garnishments.

Regular Rate of Pay

The regular rate of pay cannot be less than the minimum wage. The regular rate includes all wages for employment except certain payments excluded by the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) itself.
To see what’s not included in the regular rate please see Fact Sheet # 23: Overtime Pay Requirements of the FLSA
